Detailed Description

Maximum Torque Per Ampere (MTPA) Control component of the Motor Control SDK.

The torque of the PMSM can be expressed with the equation shown below:

\[ T_e = \frac{3}{2}\times p \times \phi \times i_q + \frac{3}{2}\times p\times(L_d-L_q)\times i_q\times i_d \]

When the motor is a surface mount permanent magnet synchronous motor (SM-PMSM), the \( L_d \) and \( L_q \) are almost the same, so only the \( i_q \) can influence the torque. For internal permanent magnet synchronous motor (I-PMSM), the \( L_d \) is not equal to \( L_q \). Both \( i_d \) and \( i_q \) will influence the torque. The aim of the MTPA (maximum-torque-per-ampere) control is to calculate the reference currents which maximize the ratio between produced electromagnetic torque and copper losses. The input of this component is the \( i_q \) reference. The output of this component is \( i_d \) reference.
